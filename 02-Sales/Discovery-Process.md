# GlenRay Systems — Standardized Discovery Process

## Overview

Every engagement begins with a structured discovery process. This ensures we understand the client's business, identify the highest-ROI automation opportunities, and set clear expectations before any work begins.

**Duration:** 1–2 calls (45 min each) + async audit  
**Output:** Automation Opportunity Report + Proposal  
**Who runs it:** Account lead or senior implementer  

---

## Phase 1: Qualification Call (15–20 min)

### Purpose
Determine if this prospect is a fit before investing discovery time.

### Script Framework

**Opening (2 min):**
> "Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. I help businesses like yours put AI to work on the stuff that eats up your team's day — the repetitive, manual work that's costing you time and money. Before I walk you through anything, I'd love to understand what's going on in your business. Sound good?"

**Discovery Questions (10 min):**

1. "Walk me through a typical day at your business. Where does your team spend most of their time?"
2. "What's the most frustrating bottleneck you deal with on a weekly basis?"
3. "Have you tried any technology or software to fix that? What happened?"
4. "If I could give you back 10 hours a week of your team's time, where would that time go?"
5. "What would solving this problem be worth to you — roughly — in a year?"
6. "Who else would need to be involved in a decision like this?"
7. "What's your timeline? Is this a 'nice to have' or a 'we need this fixed' situation?"

**Qualification Checklist:**

- [ ] Revenue above $1M/year (or >10 employees)
- [ ] Clear pain point identified
- [ ] Decision-maker on the call (or accessible)
- [ ] Budget expectation in range ($2,500+)
- [ ] Timeline is within 90 days
- [ ] Not looking for custom software development

**If qualified → Schedule Discovery Deep-Dive**  
**If not qualified → Offer workshop or digital product as alternative**

---

## Phase 2: Discovery Deep-Dive (45 min)

### Purpose
Map the client's workflows, identify automation opportunities, and gather info for the audit.

### Agenda

**1. Business Overview (10 min)**
- How many employees? Roles?
- What software/tools do they currently use?
- What does their tech stack look like? (CRM, email, scheduling, etc.)
- Annual revenue range? Growth goals?

**2. Workflow Mapping (20 min)**

For each major workflow, document:

| Workflow Element | Details |
|-----------------|---------|
| **Process name** | e.g., "New patient intake" |
| **Who does it** | Role, not name |
| **How often** | Daily / Weekly / Per event |
| **Time per occurrence** | Minutes or hours |
| **Current tools used** | Software, paper, phone, etc. |
| **Pain level (1–10)** | How frustrating is this? |
| **What goes wrong** | Errors, delays, missed steps |

**Target: Map 3–5 core workflows**

**3. Prioritization (10 min)**
- "Of everything we just discussed, which ONE thing would make the biggest difference if we fixed it first?"
- "What would success look like 90 days from now?"
- "Are there any constraints I should know about? (compliance, vendor locks, team resistance)"

**4. Next Steps (5 min)**
- "Here's what happens next: I'm going to put together an Automation Opportunity Report for you. It'll show you exactly where AI can save you time and money, with rough ROI numbers. I'll have that to you within 5 business days. Then we'll hop on a short call to walk through it and, if it makes sense, I'll put together a proposal. Sound good?"

---

## Phase 3: Automation Audit (Internal — 2–4 hours)

### Purpose
Analyze discovery findings and produce the Automation Opportunity Report.

### Steps

1. **Review discovery notes** — Verify all workflow maps are complete
2. **Score each workflow** using the Automation Opportunity Matrix (see Audit Template)
3. **Identify top 3 opportunities** ranked by ROI and implementation ease
4. **Map recommended AI tools** for each opportunity
5. **Calculate estimated ROI** (time saved × hourly cost × frequency)
6. **Draft the report** using the standard template
7. **Internal review** — Have a second set of eyes check the numbers

### Deliverable
→ Automation Opportunity Report (see `08-Templates/Automation-Audit-Template.md`)

---

## Phase 4: Proposal Presentation (30 min)

### Purpose
Walk the client through findings and close the deal.

### Structure

1. **Recap their pain** (2 min) — "Here's what you told me..."
2. **Present the findings** (10 min) — Walk through top 3 opportunities with ROI
3. **Recommend a starting point** (5 min) — Always suggest ONE use case to start
4. **Present pricing tiers** (5 min) — Starter / Growth / Premium
5. **Handle objections** (5 min)
6. **Close or set decision timeline** (3 min)

### Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "This sounds expensive" | "I understand. Let me show you what it's costing you NOT to fix this. [Reference ROI calc]. Most clients see payback within 60 days." |
| "We tried something like this before" | "What happened? [Listen]. That's actually common with generic tools. We build specifically for [their industry] — that's the difference." |
| "My team won't use it" | "That's exactly why we include training and a 30/90 day support window. We don't just set it up and walk away." |
| "Can I think about it?" | "Of course. What specifically would you want to think through? I want to make sure I've answered everything." |
| "I need to talk to my partner" | "Totally get it. Would it help if I joined a quick call with both of you? Sometimes it's easier when I can answer their questions directly." |
