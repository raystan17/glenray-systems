# GlenRay Systems — Service Packaging Framework

## Overview

This framework turns every automation use case into a sellable, packaged service. It integrates with the business consultant prompt to ensure every service we offer has a clear name, deliverables, timeline, pricing, sales pitch, and value proposition.

**Rule:** If a use case can't be packaged using this framework, it's too custom for our model. Move on.

---

## Packaging Template

For every service we productize, complete this template:

### 1. Service Name
A clear, benefit-driven name a non-technical person would understand.
- Good: "AI Lead Rescue" / "Smart Inbox Manager" / "No-Show Shield"
- Bad: "NLP Email Classification Pipeline" / "Workflow Automation Suite"

### 2. What's Included (Deliverables)
Bullet list of exactly what the client gets. Be specific enough that scope is clear, vague enough to allow implementation flexibility.

### 3. Timeline
How long from kickoff to live. Use our standard phases:
- Starter: 2–3 weeks
- Growth: 3–4 weeks
- Premium: 4–6 weeks

### 4. Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Scope | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|-------|----------|
| **Starter** | Single use case, minimal integrations | $2,500–$5,000 | First-time buyers testing the water |
| **Growth** | Multi-use case, full integrations | $7,500–$15,000 | Committed operators ready for change |
| **Premium** | Full suite, training, ongoing optimization | $20,000–$50,000 | Businesses going all-in on efficiency |

### 5. Sales Pitch
A 3–4 sentence pitch written for a skeptical 55-year-old business owner. No jargon. Focus on pain → cost → solution → result.

### 6. One-Line Value Proposition
A single sentence that answers: "Why should I pay for this?"

## Packaging Process

```
1. Identify use case from our 15 standard use cases
   ↓
2. Fill in the Service Packaging Template
   ↓
3. Review: Can a junior explain this in 2 minutes?
   ↓
4. Review: Is the ROI obvious to a non-technical buyer?
   ↓
5. Review: Can we deliver this in the stated timeline with our SOPs?
   ↓
6. Approved → Add to service catalog
   ↓
7. Create supporting materials (one-pager, proposal snippet, case study template)
```

---

## Pricing Philosophy

1. **Price on value, not hours** — If it saves them $50K/year, a $10K investment is cheap
2. **Starter tier removes risk** — Low entry point to prove value, then expand
3. **Growth tier is the sweet spot** — This is where most clients should land
4. **Premium tier is for expansion** — Existing clients who want everything
5. **Never discount, always de-scope** — If budget is tight, reduce scope to fit
